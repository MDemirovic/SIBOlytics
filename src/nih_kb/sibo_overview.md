# SIBO Overview
Source: https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/digestive-diseases/small-intestinal-bacterial-overgrowth-sibo

Small intestinal bacterial overgrowth (SIBO) is a condition in which there is an abnormal increase in the overall bacterial population in the small intestine. It is often characterized by symptoms such as bloating, abdominal pain, diarrhea, and malabsorption. SIBO can occur when the normal mechanisms that control bacterial populations in the small intestine, such as gastric acid secretion and intestinal motility (the migrating motor complex), are disrupted.
