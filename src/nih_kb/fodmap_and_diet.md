# FODMAP and Diet
Source: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3966170/

A low-FODMAP diet is often recommended to manage symptoms of SIBO and IBS. FODMAPs are fermentable oligosaccharides, disaccharides, monosaccharides, and polyols—short-chain carbohydrates that are poorly absorbed in the small intestine. When these carbohydrates reach the bacteria in the gut, they are rapidly fermented, producing gas and drawing water into the intestine, which can exacerbate symptoms like bloating and diarrhea.
